# Gardening Readiness Widget

This repository hosts a **public‑facing gardening readiness widget** developed by the **Global Institute for Water Security (GIWS)** at the University of Saskatchewan.

The widget provides **at‑a‑glance context** on spring gardening conditions based on recent weather trends and seasonal climate norms in Saskatchewan. It is intended to support general awareness, not to provide planting advice or predictions.

---

## What this widget does

At a glance, the widget summarizes:
- Whether recent weather conditions are **not yet suitable**, **improving**, or **generally suitable for cold‑tolerant garden crops**
- Seasonal context related to soil warming and frost risk
- The date the information was last updated

The focus is on **conditions**, not recommendations.

---

## What this widget does not do

- It does **not** measure soil temperature directly
- It does **not** predict future weather or frost events
- It does **not** provide crop‑specific planting advice

Local conditions can vary due to soil type, drainage, snow cover, and microclimate.

---

## Data and interpretation

The widget relies on:
- Recent air temperature trends as a proxy for soil warming
- Seasonal climate context (e.g., typical frost timing)
- Recent precipitation for general soil workability context

All data sources are public, and interpretation thresholds are deliberately conservative to avoid over‑confidence or misinterpretation.
